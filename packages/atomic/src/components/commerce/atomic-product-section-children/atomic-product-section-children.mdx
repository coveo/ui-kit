## Usage
This section is meant to render child products, available when using the `<atomic-product-children>` component.
It displays related products, variants, accessories, or bundle options that are associated with the main product.

**Key Features:**
- Shows children at the bottom of the result, visually separated from the main product
- Content is indented and wrapped in a border to distinguish it from the parent product
- Typically used for displaying product bundles, accessories, variants, or related items
- Automatically hides when empty (no child content)

### Example within a Product Template
```html
<atomic-commerce-product-list>
  <atomic-product-template>
    <template>
      <atomic-product-section-visual>
        <atomic-product-image field="ec_thumbnails"></atomic-product-image>
      </atomic-product-section-visual>

      <atomic-product-section-name>
        <atomic-product-link class="font-bold"></atomic-product-link>
      </atomic-product-section-name>

      <atomic-product-section-emphasized>
        <atomic-product-price currency="USD"></atomic-product-price>
      </atomic-product-section-emphasized>

      <atomic-product-section-children>
        <atomic-product-children>
          <!-- Child products will be rendered here -->
        </atomic-product-children>
      </atomic-product-section-children>
    </template>
  </atomic-product-template>
</atomic-commerce-product-list>
```

### Common Use Cases
- **Product Bundles**: Display items that come together as a package
- **Accessories**: Show compatible accessories or add-ons
- **Product Variants**: List different colors, sizes, or configurations
- **Related Products**: Display complementary or alternative items
- **Upsell/Cross-sell**: Present premium options or additional products

### Styling Guidelines
- Use indentation (margin-left) to visually separate child products from the parent
- Apply borders or background colors to clearly distinguish the child section
- Keep child product information concise to avoid overwhelming the user
- Consider using smaller font sizes for child product details
- Maintain visual hierarchy with the parent product remaining prominent

### Content Structure Recommendations
- Lead with a clear heading that describes the relationship (e.g., "Bundle Options", "Available in")
- List child products in a logical order (price, popularity, relevance)
- Include pricing information when relevant
- Use consistent formatting for all child items
